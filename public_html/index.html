FAQs

How do we get there?

While we don’t recommend walking, you should probably start no later than late May and pack lots of sunscreen, a tent, and water.

If you are driving, Google gives great directions. Start by putting in your address and then make Joseph, Oregon your destination.

If you are flying, Walla Walla Airport is the closest to Joseph, about 2.5 hours away. Obviously at this distance renting a car is a must. The next airport is Boise (a little over 4 hours), then Portland (about 5.5 hours).

There are smaller regional airports similar to Walla Walla in Lewiston, ID and the Tri Cities area, OR. Just like with Walla Walla, there are no direct flights and you will have to fly into Portland, Seattle, or Boise and catch a connecting flight to one of them. Kelly flies with Alaska Airlines and has never had a bad experience.

Don’t forget your rental car! Book this at the same time you are booking your hotel and flights. You want to make sure you have a way to get there as well as a place to stay.

Where can we stay?

There is a lot of lodging in Joseph, everything from vacation rentals at the lake to motels to B&Bs. However, if you are planning on coming, we recommend booking as soon as possible as some places are already completely booked.

The next town over is Enterprise, you can find some accommodations there as well. If you are having trouble deciding or finding a place, give Kelly or Karen a call and we’d be happy to help you find something.

What is there to do around town?

Drinks and Food

There are three breweries in the area (all of which have food):

  1. Terminal Gravity Brewing-Enterprise
  2. Mutiny Brewing Company-Joseph
  3. Embers Brew House-Joseph

There is also Lathrop Country Wines in Joseph and just down Main Street from Kelly’s favorite breakfast spot, Red Horse Coffee (their savory corncake is divine!), is Stein Distillery.

Other spots to catch a bite to eat are Coco’s Grill and Beech 18 Bar and The Outlaw.

Shopping and Galleries

There are a few art galleries, a statue garden, and many cute shops to walk around in. Don’t forget to stop by Arrowhead Chocolates for some delicious truffles and a hazelnut milk chai.

Recreation

For the more adventurous, there are kayak, paddleboard, canoe, and rowboat rentals at Wallowa Lake. There are also many hiking trails in the Eagle Cap Wilderness.

Craving something a little more thrilling? Take a ride up the Wallowa Lake Tramway on a 3700 vertical foot ascent to the top of Mt. Howard.
